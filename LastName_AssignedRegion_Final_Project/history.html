Our History & Culture

A Brief History

The PNW was originally home to a large number of Indigenous groups, including the Coast Salish, Chinook, and Nez Perce, who thrived on the abundant marine resources and forests. Their rich cultures, based on salmon fishing and cedar wood carving, still influence the region today.

In the 18th and 19th centuries, European and American explorers arrived, drawn by the lucrative fur trade and timber. The subsequent establishment of ports like Seattle and Portland cemented the region's importance as a gateway to Asia, leading to rapid growth driven by the Gold Rush and the lumber industry.

Cultural Highlights

Festivals

  • **Bumbershoot:** A major annual music and arts festival in Seattle.
  • **Portland Rose Festival:** A traditional civic celebration held since 1907.
  • **Northwest Folklife Festival:** Celebrates the diverse cultures of the Pacific Northwest.

Traditional Crafts & Foods

  • **Traditional Crafts:** Totem Poles, carved cedar canoes, and intricate woven basketry from Indigenous artists.
  • **Local Foods:** Wild Pacific Salmon, Dungeness Crab, berries (Blueberries, Blackberries), and locally sourced Oysters.
  • **Modern Staples:** Craft coffee, microbrewed beer, and farm-to-table cuisine.
